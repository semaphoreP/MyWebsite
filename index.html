Research

Research Experience

At Cornell, I worked with Professor Terry Herter on FORCAST, a mid-infrared camera for SOFIA. I am thrilled to have the unique opportunity to fly on SOFIA three times and am forever immortalized by having my elbow poking into the left edge of this NASA picutre and by a cameo in the SOFIA safety video.

Science wise, my work on FORCAST has invovled working on calibrating FORCAST, improving the reduction pipeline (if you ever want to talk about median filtering, I'm your man), and studying the atmosphere of Jupiter.

My research on Jupiter also involved Professor Peter Gierasch. We used FORCAST images of Jupiter to look at ortho-para hydorgen ratios and how they vary across Jupiter. The ortho-para ratio is a good tracer for convection on Jupiter and we show that FORCAST is capable of performing this measurement.
