Research

Past Research

At Cornell, I worked with Professor Terry Herter on FORCAST, a mid-infrared camera for SOFIA. I am thrilled to have the unique opportunity to fly on SOFIA three times. Science wise, my work on FORCAST has invovled working on calibrating FORCAST, improving the reduction pipeline, and studying the atmosphere of Jupiter.

My research on Jupiter also involved Professor Peter Gierasch. We used FORCAST images of Jupiter to look at ortho-para hydorgen ratios and how they vary across Jupiter. The ortho-para ratio is a good tracer for convection on Jupiter and we want to show that FORCAST is capable of performing this measurement.

Current Research

I have just started working on the Gemini Planet Imager, a near-infrared instrument that will directly image and take spectra of young Jovian-sized planets while they are still forming. I am currently working on characterizing the instrument, and in particular, working on astrometric and spectrophotometric calibration.

Service

Zambia Community Education Initiative (ZCEI)

The ZCEI is a 501(c)3-pending not-for-profit organization that aims to expand access to education in Zambia. Curently, the ZCEI is working with the Temba and Julia Ndulo Foundation to raise funds to build a school in in rural Zambia. We firmly believe in Zambians helping Zambians, so the school project was conceived by Zambians, is being coordinated by Zambians, and will be built by Zambians. Our organization is working on assisting them with donations and spreading awareness. It has been a great experience seeing a project from when it was just being conceived to when much progress is happening. Also, through the ZCEI, I have had the chance to see meet the locals and see the beauty of Zambia!

Cornell Computer Reuse Association (CCRA)

The CCRA is a student organization that collects, refurbishes, and donates used computers to schools, orphanages, and community centers all around the world. I have been involved with the CCRA ever since the start of my undergraduate career at Cornell. In total, we have donated over 2000 computers at a rate of over 200 computers per year. I have gotten to know a lot of amazing people and have gotten the opportunity to share in some very unique experiences. In my four years in the club, I have helped organize a service trip to Grand Bahama to install and check up on computers, attended and talked at two conferences to promote our cause, and have spent countless hours refurbishing computers. I am always interesed in expanding education and computer access throughout the world, so contact me if you are interested!
